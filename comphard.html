  • Everything about
    Computer Hardware

    An Article by Maninder Kaur

  • Introduction

    If you’re about to make you first computer and need to know what to get, or need to fix you current computer, it does help to know the hardware inside the amazing device. In this article, we will explore everything you need to know about computer hardware, from the basic components to the latest advancements. Whether you're a tech enthusiast or a curious beginner, join us on this journey to unravel the intricacies of computer hardware.

  • CPU

    The Central Processing Unit or CPU is known as the “brain” of the computer. Every single operation done on your computer is first gone through the CPU to execute decision-making and later carry out the operations. After executions it often stores the operation in its memory. Just like in the human body, the CPU controls all actions of other parts of hardware.

  • RAM

    The Random Access Memory or RAM is the temporary storage unit for the computers data. All data that is stored within the RAM can be accessed by other hardware units to be used. The RAM also serves as a temporary workspace for applications. When you work on a document, edit an image, or play a video game, the relevant data is loaded into RAM for faster access and manipulation. Also works with data transfer.

  • Other Storage

    Other Storage Units like Hard Disk Drives, HDDs, and Solid State Drives, SDDs, are more percent storage units for computer data. HDDs are the primary place in the computer for long-term memory storage. You operating system, application, multimedia files and other important pieces of data are stored in the HDDs. SDDs soon the other hand are for smaller, short-term memory usage. They often use flash memory technology and are significantly faster in data retrieval then the HDDs

  • Motherboard

    The Motherboard works as a hub of communication between all other parts of the hardware in the computer. It’s like a highway that causes data to be able to travel far and wide across the computer. The motherboard provides pathways for communication between different hardware competes such as the CPU, RAM, HDDs, SDDs, and others. All actions, data, and power is spread through the gateways of the motherboard.

  • GPU

    5. The Graphics Processing Unit or the GPU specializes in the rendering of your computer graphics on your computer monitor. The GPU is tasked with anything related with your computer pixels, from CAD to your video game graphics. The GPU is also responsible for anything related with videos, like editing, transcribing and playback.

  • PSU

    The Power Supply Unit or PSU is here to make this all work out. All the power needed to keep a computer running works with the PSU. It all starts with the wall outlet, and the wires that connect the computer to it. Electricity flows through the PSU and then proceeds to travel through the wires until it is in the computer’s PSU. Now the PSU transfers the energy through the motherboard, and fires up every component inside the computer.

  • Cooling

    The Cooling Systems are in the computer to ensure that the components do not overheat. The power that is given to each component can cause immense heat to erupt from them. Without the cooling systems, these components end up fried or even worse, bursting the computer into flames. Older, much larger, computers had a reputation for getting fried, so fans would be installed in the rooms to ensure that the computer would not burn. Now, the fans are inside the computer.

  • Conclusion

    That is the basics of computer hardware. Each component has its own mini components that have their own purpose. At the end of the day, knowing what’s inside your computer is crucial. Programmers, engineers, and others alike, it should be common knowledge, to be able to identify, fix and analyze the machine many spend hours upon hours on.
