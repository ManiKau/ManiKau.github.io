  • Skateboarding:
    A Culture

    An Article by Maninder Kaur

  • Introduction

    I fall about 20 times per day. This might seem like a lot, but I’m a skateboarder. The definition of skateboarding according to Google, is “ the sport or pastime of riding on a skateboard”. I have pursued skateboarding since I was 8, and have loved the thrill of the tricks, the joy of landing a flip, and the congregation of my fellow friends at the skatepark. Let’s look at this life, by diving deep into the culture and its importance.

  • History

    In the 1950s and ’60s, surfers in California starting theorizing of “sidewalk surfing”. They envisioned being able to glide across the streets in the same ways they did on the waves. The first prototype: a pair of roller skates attached to wooden planks. After years of further experimenting, the 1970s saw the first commercial skateboards (thanks to companies like Makaha and Hobie), and along with it, the surge of popularity.

  • Creativity

    Skateboarding is a culture of creativity. Just like how every artist has their own style, skateboarders do too. Rodney Mullen, was so innovative in his tricks, he was dubbed the “Godfather of Street Skating”. He incorporated technical and intricate flatland tricks to further revolutionize freestyle skateboarding. Danny Way on the other hand was known for pushing boundaries and doing what was dubbed impossible.

  • Community

    Skateboarding doesn’t just stop at sports, but expresses itself in many other forms. For example, Shepard Fairly is an artist who regularly includes skateboarding imagery into his art pieces. He ferries skateboarding culture, graphics, people and more. Another artistic pursuit, is fashion, and the streetwear brand Supreme also incorporates skateboarding into its clothes.   • Conclusion

    Skateboarding is a culture has a rich history, has creative aspects for each that try it, brings people together, has integrated itself within many fields. When I skate I remember all the people before me who tried this exact trick, maybe in the exact place. It makes me feel happy, knowing this culture.
