    An Article by Maninder Kaur

  • Introduction

    In the early hours of March 1st, 1980, the French mathematician, Benoit Mandelbrot, became awe-struck by the computer print he held in his hands. His workplace's computer had been used by him in order to graph a mathematical set, and he had initially suspected a simple function would be printed. As he zoomed in closer, instead of finding a simple graph, he found an astonishing fractal. It repeated itself over and over as the zoom increased. Mandelbrot had just made a discovery that would revolutionize the world: he had discovered the Mandelbrot set, a discovery that would make history.

  • One

    As a result of the discovery of the Mandelbrot set, the fractals and its applications have revolutionized the study of mathematics opening new avenues of exploration.

  • Two

    However, how particularly does the Mandelbrot set work? IBM, the place it all started, explained that the Mandelbrot set is generated by a simple mathematical formula, but its results are anything but simple. The formula takes a complex number, which is a number with both real and imaginary components, and iterates it over and over again. The resulting pattern is a stunning display of infinite complexity, with intricate shapes and details emerging as you zoom in closer and closer.

  • Three

    The Mandelbrot set has many fascinating properties that is being studied by mathematicians to this day. One of these properties is the concept of self-similarity according to Joe Horgan, a journalist for Scientific American, meaning that the pattern of the set is repeated at various levels of magnification. This self-similarity is what gives the Mandelbrot set its fractal-like appearance.

  • Four

    In addition to its beauty, the Mandelbrot set has profound implications for the field of mathematics. It has been used to explore the concept of chaos theory, which studies the behavior of systems that are sensitive to initial conditions.

  • Four

    Yet, the applications of the Mandelbrot set don’t stop at mathematics. It has inspired artists who have used the set as a source of inspiration for their work. One such artist is Hamid Naderi Yeganeh, an Iranian mathematical artist. He uses mathematical functions and sets to create elaborate and beautiful pieces are art.

  • Five

    The Mandelbrot set was just one mathematical set that inspired him. It has also been used in computer graphics and video games, where it is employed to generate complex and realistic landscapes, by randomly generated numbers from the set. If you’ve ever played video games like Minecraft or Terraria, then the terrain that you see was randomly generated based on… you guessed it, numbers from the Mandelbrot set.

  • Conclusion

    The Mandelbrot set is a fascinating and influential object in the field of not only mathematics. Its production by Benoit Mandelbrot was a major milestone in the study of fractals and their applications. From its intricate beauty to its implications from chaos theory, art, and video games, the Mandelbrot set continues to captivate mathematicians and non-mathematicians alike.
