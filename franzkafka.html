    An Article by Maninder Kaur

  • Introduction

    I recently read the book Metamorphosis by Franz Kafka and… Oh. My. God. Initially it seemed so absurd. My first thoughts were, “Cool, dude got turned into a cockroach”. But now I understand that the book is not about a bug, but about the society in which we live in. Here are the messages that I learned from the book.

  • Identity

    The struggle one has with their identity is heavily showcased in Metamorphosis. Gregor Samsa, the main character gets turned into a life-size bug and must struggle with this. Not only him, but the people around him such as his family, become estranged. We as humans struggle with ourselves all the time when something happens, from our sexuality to gender to religion.

  • Isolation

    Isolation and alienation is present the book. In society itself, when one does not fit within the confines of societal norms, they are pushed away and alienated, just like Gregor. He is now a bug and people start treating him terribly. It shows human nature and how when one is a burden to us, we will not hesitate to leave them and treat them differently.

  • Normality

    Despite being turned into a gigantic bug, Gregor still tries to continue his job and provide for his family. He tries to redeem himself. Human nature is to create a sense of normalcy, even futile attempts to continue your job, for example. Gregor is an example of how we all try to seem normal in this absurd and disastrous world.

  • Absurdity

    Life is absurd. In our very chaotic word, anything can happen. Every single day we read of things that seem fake, but are 100% real. Metamorphosis explores this by observing the sheer absurdity of life. A man wakes up to only become a bug. Absurd.
