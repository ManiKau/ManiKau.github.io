  • Maninder's Must
    Read Sci-Fi

    An Article by Maninder Kaur

  • Introduction

    From a very young age, I LOVED reading. From mysteries to historical fiction, you could bet that I would my nose in a book every day. Yet, despite all these genres, there is one that I have always had a soft spot for. Science Fiction. The dystopian cities and the space battles stole my heart. So here, I’ll share some of my favorite science fiction books, you too should read.

  • For Robot Lovers

    Do Android’s Dream of Electric Sheep by Philip K. Dick is a classic. For any of you movie buffs, this book inspired the Bladerunner movie! The book follows Rick Deckard, a bounty hunter in a futuristic world where he must kill androids. Dick has a way of describing the characters, the environment, and overall keeping you on your toes.

  • For VR Fanatics

    Warcross by Marie Lu follows a young bounty hunter named Emika. She uses her hacking skills to arrest criminals. But then, she gets picked by a billionaire named Hideo, a creator of a virtual reality game, to fix a bug, AND compete in the video game. The amount of twists, turns and shocks are infinite. It all hits you like a train. It also has a real representation of hacking and has a CS GIRLIE!

  • Bedtime Story

    A Wrinkle in Time by Madeline L’Engle is a book every kid should read. It follows two young siblings they embark on an adventure through time and space, to save their father who has been captured by a monster who threaten to hurt all the worlds. It a perfect bedtime story and extremely enchanting.

  • All Over the Place

    Slaughterhouse Five by Kurt Vonnegut follows the life of Billy Pilgrim, a war veteran who takes the readers through a journey through war. He occasionally goes through time, gets kidnapped by aliens, and has hallucinations. It’s a little hard to read, but a must-read.

  • A CLASSIC

    Frankenstein by Mary Shelley STARTED the mad scientist trope. It follows three characters and unfolds the story of a scientist bent on resurrecting the dead… only to create a monster. You sympathize with many and learn about the ethics that comes with science.
